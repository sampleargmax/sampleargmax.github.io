About Me

Currently, my research focuses on LLM post-training techniques and algorithms, with a specific emphasis on search agent, tool use, reasoning, and instruction following. My expertise spans the full LLM pipeline—from training and inference to evaluation—covering key areas such as RLVR, reward modeling, data synthesis, and multi-domain training. I have extensive hands-on experience with large-scale GPU clusters (~15M GPU hours) and end-to-end training of multiple 100B+ parameter foundation models. Prior to this, I completed my Ph.D. with a focus on applying deep reinforcement learning (DRL) to cloud environment scheduling challenges. My doctoral work aimed at utilizing neural network generalization for decision-making to optimize solution flexibility and reduce response times, drawing extensively on MDP modeling, uplift modeling, and online/offline DRL.
